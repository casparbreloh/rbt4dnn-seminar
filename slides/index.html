01 · ProblemWhy requirements?

Accuracy answers how often.
Requirements ask when.

A model can perform well on a test set while violating a necessary condition in a rare, safety-relevant scenario.

Conventional testingTest set → aggregate metric

Summarizes behavior over the sampled distribution.

Requirements-based testingScenario → expected behavior

Tests a stated input condition against its postcondition.

Formal semanticsTwo requirement classes

The natural language has a mathematical contract underneath.

CSFR · SEMANTIC ROBUSTNESS
∀x ∈ X: φ(x) ⇒ ∀i ∈ I: N(x) = N(x ⊕ i)

Within a semantic scenario φ, irrelevant feature variation i must not change the model output.

SFFR · FUNCTIONAL BEHAVIOR
∀x ∈ X: φX(x) ⇒ φY(N(x))

Whenever the input precondition holds, the learned component’s output must satisfy the postcondition.

Used in the paper: MNIST and CelebA-HQ instantiate CSFR; SGSM and ImageNet instantiate SFFR.

Training detailFiltering + low-rank adaptation

Each requirement creates a small training problem around a frozen generator.

FILTERED TRAINING PAIRS
DpᵢX = {(x,pᵢ) | φiX(gt(x))}

Keep only images whose glossary labels satisfy precondition pᵢ; pair every image with that precondition text.

LORA UPDATE
W′ = W + BA,   rank(BA)=r ≪ min(d,k)

Freeze the 12B-parameter FLUX backbone W; train only low-rank matrices A and B.

Trade-off: focus raises precondition consistency, but one adapter per requirement makes training and maintenance scale linearly.

Threats to validityThe semantic measurement loop

The central uncertainty is whether the generated scene means what the test says.

LABEL

What is present?

Scene graphs, morphology, human labels, or VQA define glossary terms.

MEASURE

Does it match?

Glossary-term classifiers operationalize consistency and diversity.

Is it credible?

Experienced problems + lessons learnedWhat the experiments changed in my interpretation

Generating an image was easy. Establishing evidence was the hard part.

01 · ARTIFACT

available ≠ reproducible

Released fields support a result check, but not fresh 12B-model training without the full data, weights, configuration, and compute path.

02 · ORACLE

evaluator quality caps evidence

The weak CelebA attribute classifier made .613 a caution signal—not a trustworthy generator benchmark.

03 · AGGREGATION

means hide local failure

The shared MNIST generator averages .945, while its hardest requirement M3 falls to .743.

Lesson: report requirement-level uncertainty and provenance before celebrating an aggregate score.

RecommendationsWhat a stronger RBT4DNN study should do next

Turn a promising generator into a defensible assurance process.

01

Joint labels

Record precondition validity and postcondition failure on the same generated image, with assessor agreement.

02

Coverage

Measure systematic semantic or latent-space coverage, not only realism and average diversity.

03

Cost-aware training

Compare per-requirement, shared, and conditional generators by valid-failure yield per unit compute.

04

Full provenance

Release seeds, exact subsets, weights, evaluator checkpoints, and machine-readable requirement traces.

TakeawayRBT4DNN

Requirements make tests relevant.
Validity makes failures credible.

The paper’s contribution is traceability from a semantic requirement to generated evidence. The remaining research problem is proving that the evidence really belongs to that requirement.
